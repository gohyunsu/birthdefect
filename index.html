01 · Problem and task

Clinical screening needs more than a frame classifier.

Congenital heart disease is structurally diverse, rare by subtype, and difficult to identify in noisy ultrasound. The project treats screening as a patient/video-level decision supported by standard-view recognition and anatomical structure analysis.

Task A

View & quality

Recognize clinically meaningful views and reject unreliable frames.

Task B

Video screening

Aggregate information across a cardiac sequence for normal/abnormal prediction.

Task C

Structure

Segment or localize cardiac structures as an interpretable auxiliary task.

04 · Evaluation protocol

Make a screening claim only with defensible evaluation.

Splits

Patient-level splits; when available, device/site-held-out testing. Never split frames randomly across train and test.

Metrics

AUROC, AUPRC, sensitivity at fixed specificity, confidence intervals, calibration, and per-class recall.

Ablations

Single frame, temporal pooling, attention/SELSA, auxiliary anatomy, and semi-supervised learning.

Safety

Inspect false negatives, uncertain predictions, subgroup shifts, annotation noise, and clinical failure modes.
